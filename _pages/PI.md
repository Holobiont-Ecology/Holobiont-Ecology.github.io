## Prof. Soon-Jae Lee

Prof. Soon Jae Lee investigates the principles that govern symbiotic systems, focusing on how genetic, ecological, and temporal processes integrate across scales to shape the function and evolution of holobionts. His work combines **systems biology**, **microbial ecology**, and **mycorrhiza genetics** to uncover how plants and their microbial partners coordinate metabolism, development, and chronobiology in space and time. Trained in both Physics and Biology, he approaches living systems through a quantitative and integrative lens, bridging theoretical and experimental perspectives.

During his PhD at the Plant Biology Research Institute (Montreal Botanical Garden & University of Montreal, Canada), he pioneered **RNA interference** and **circadian clock** studies in **arbuscular mycorrhizal fungi**. This work introduced the concept that **the holobiont, rather than the individual organism, constitutes the fundamental unit of chronobiology**.

At the University of Lausanne (Switzerland), he expanded this framework through mentoring, collaborative projects within the Swiss National Centre of Microbiome Research, and international initiatives. He represents arid and temperate climate sectors in the World Mycorrhizal Research Initiative and contributed to establishing the first mycorrhiza research program at the University of Latvia. His research informs emerging strategies in sustainable agriculture, ecosystem resilience, and microbiome based technologies.

### Teaching and Mentorship
Prof. Lee has taught and supervised graduate students in ecology and evolution at the University of Lausanne, where he received recognition for outstanding dedication to teaching. He is committed to strengthening scientific capacity globally, including collaborative initiatives in Africa addressing ecological and agricultural challenges in rapidly changing environments.

### Service and Scientific Leadership
He serves as an expert consultant for the National Foundation for Science and Creativity (Korea) and as editorial board member for academic journals including Springer Nature, Frontiers, and Agriculture. He is a scientific advisory board member for a UNICEF award winning international company and regularly delivers academic and public lectures in Canada, Switzerland, Latvia, and Korea.
